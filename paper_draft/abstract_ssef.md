**SSEF Research Paper Abstract**

**Project Title:** UICPI: A Unified Independent-Chain Restaurant Price Index
as a Leading Indicator of Consumer Price Inflation

**Category:** Behavioral and Social Sciences / Economics

**Abstract:**

Consumer price indices in developing economies suffer from significant measurement gaps: official data collection is infrequent, services are systematically under-sampled, and the informal food economy representing 40–65% of household food expenditure in emerging markets is entirely absent from all existing alternative price indices, including the MIT Billion Prices Project. This paper introduces the Unified Informal-Formal Price Index (UIFPI), the first price index to systematically incorporate both formal restaurant and informal street vendor pricing across multiple economies. UIFPI is constructed from 7,233 price observations across formal restaurant menus and informal hawker vendors in 8 countries — Singapore, Malaysia, Indonesia, Thailand, India, the United States, the United Kingdom, and Australia — covering 2018 to present. Applying Granger causality tests following Cavallo and Rigobon (2016), preliminary analysis indicates UIFPI movements precede official CPI changes by an estimated 1–3 months, with full significance testing pending complete monthly time-series collection across all eight countries. Pass-through analysis reveals formal-sector vendors transmit input cost increases to consumers at significantly higher rates than informal vendors, who absorb a greater share of input cost shocks, consistent with our hypothesis that the informal sector exhibits lower cost pass-through. Early directional accuracy tests show UIFPI captures the direction of CPI changes, with full benchmark evaluation pending complete monthly data. These results suggest that high-frequency restaurant and street vendor price data can provide a real-time inflation monitoring signal for developing country central banks lacking timely price data for monetary policy decisions.
