UIFPI FORMAL/INFORMAL SECTOR CLASSIFICATION — RATIONALE
Date: 2026-06-13
=========================================================

SECTION 3.4 — OPERATIONAL DEFINITION OF INFORMAL FOOD SECTOR

Definition (verbatim):
"Informal sector: food vendors with hawker or street food origins, operating from a
single stall or maximum 3 outlets at time of first price observation, primarily serving
traditional local dishes without full table service."

This definition follows Gerxhani (2004) on the role of entry barriers and scale in
distinguishing informal from formal food retail, adapted to the Southeast Asian hawker
context described in Bhaskaran & Sukumar (2007). The 3-outlet ceiling is operationalised
from the date of first price observation in this dataset (earliest collection dates range
from 2022-01 to 2024-06 depending on source).

=========================================================
PER-RESTAURANT JUSTIFICATION — SINGAPORE
=========================================================

FORMAL (sector='formal') — classified as formal despite hawker-origin branding
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

1. Song Fa Bak Kut Teh
   Current sector: formal (updated 2026-06-13, was: informal)
   Rationale: Founded 1969 as a push-cart vendor; by 2022, operated 30+ sit-down
   air-conditioned restaurant branches across Singapore plus international outlets
   (China, Cambodia). Full table service with set menus and a la carte pricing.
   Exceeds 3-outlet threshold by a factor of 10. Classification: FORMAL.

2. A Noodle Story
   Current sector: formal (updated 2026-06-13, was: informal)
   Rationale: Founded 2013 at Amoy Street Food Centre; received Michelin Bib Gourmand
   recognition and subsequently expanded to multiple hawker centre branches and a
   dine-in concept. By 2022, exceeded 3 operating locations. The Amoy Food Centre
   stall coexists with expanded outlets, but the restaurant entity as observed in this
   dataset spans multiple locations. Classification: FORMAL (borderline — primary stall
   remains at hawker centre, but chain operations disqualify from informal definition).

3. Old Chang Kee
   Current sector: formal (no change required)
   Rationale: SGX-listed company (OCK:SP) with 100+ retail kiosks and outlets across
   Singapore and Malaysia since IPO in 2008. Franchise model. Classification: FORMAL.

4. BreadTalk
   Current sector: formal (no change required)
   Rationale: SGX-listed (BREAD:SP), 800+ outlets across 17 countries as of 2022.
   Global franchise bakery chain; no hawker origins. Classification: FORMAL.

5. Toast Box
   Current sector: formal (no change required)
   Rationale: Wholly owned by BreadTalk Group; operates 80+ kopi tiam-concept outlets
   across Singapore, Malaysia, Indonesia, China. Corporate chain, not hawker.
   Classification: FORMAL.

6. Crystal Jade GO
   Current sector: formal (no change required)
   Rationale: Subsidiary of Crystal Jade Culinary Concepts; part of 30+ outlet chain
   across Asia. Operates as express-format counterpart to Crystal Jade restaurants
   (formal dining chain). Classification: FORMAL.

HAWKER CHAN — Note:
   Not found in database (0 rows). Hawker Chan (Liao Fan Hawker Chan) holds a single
   Michelin star for its Chinatown Complex stall, but has expanded to 8+ international
   franchises. If added, would be classified FORMAL under the 3-outlet rule. Currently
   excluded due to no price observations collected.

=========================================================
SECTOR DISTRIBUTION (post-reclassification, 2026-06-13)
=========================================================

Singapore:
  formal:   5,527 rows  (includes BreadTalk 440, Toast Box 300, Old Chang Kee 78,
                          Song Fa 88, A Noodle Story 44, Crystal Jade GO 64, others)
  informal:   ~1,000 rows (hawker centre stalls, push-cart vendors, single outlets)

The formal/informal split reflects Singapore's well-documented hawker-to-chain pipeline,
where successful street food vendors frequently formalise over time (Lim 2019;
Singapore Tourism Board 2022). This reclassification aligns our data with the definitional
threshold rather than origin mythology.
