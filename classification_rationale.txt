UIFPI — FORMAL / INFORMAL CLASSIFICATION RATIONALE
(Section 3.4 source — research paper)
Last updated: 2026-06-13
================================================================

1. DEFINITION
-------------
Informal sector: food vendors with hawker or street food origins,
operating from a single stall or a maximum of three outlets at the
time of the first price observation, primarily serving traditional
local dishes without full table service.

Formal sector: restaurant chains and casual / full-service
establishments that (a) operate four or more outlets at the time
of the first price observation, OR (b) provide full table service
with printed menus, taxed receipts, and a registered company entity,
OR (c) are publicly listed or operate as franchised entities.

2. APPLICATION NOTES
--------------------
- Outlet count is measured at the time of the *first* observation in
  the dataset. A vendor that later expands above the threshold keeps
  its initial classification to preserve the time-series integrity
  of the informal index.
- Brand recognition outside the home market (e.g., international
  franchise rollout) automatically promotes a vendor to formal even
  if the home-country outlet count is small.
- TripAdvisor / Yelp listings are not used as a classification signal.
  Number of outlets and presence of full table service are determined
  from each vendor's own website or press coverage.
- Items priced in non-local currency on the menu (USD-denominated
  Singapore hotel restaurants, GBP menus in luxury London hotels) are
  classified formal by default.

6. JUSTIFICATION FOR INFORMAL CLASSIFICATIONS (sample)
------------------------------------------------------
  India informal vendors: Mumbai street-vendor stalls, Delhi chaat
    counters, single-shop South Indian tiffin spots — all single-outlet
    or family-run.
  Australia informal: independent cafés, food-court stalls, single-outlet
    restaurants documented in OpenStreetMap / Yelp local listings.
  UK informal: independent takeaway and café establishments, no chain
    presence beyond a single location.
  US informal: NYC food carts, food trucks, single-outlet diners.
  Singapore informal: hawker stall vendors documented in NHB hawker
    surveys, NEA stall listings, and Wayback-indexed hawker centre menus.
  Malaysia informal: kopitiams and single-outlet mamak stalls.
